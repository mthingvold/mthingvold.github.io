Taphonomy

The study of the processes that affects an organism from the moment they die to their fossilization and subsequent discovery

one of the main goals of taphonomy is to better understand biases in the fossil record Because of the fossilization process not all organisms have the same chance of being preservered ( a bias )

Body fossils Most hard parts are not solid material, but porous. Pour space is occupied by organic material in life, and upon death decays. - hard parts made of: - Carbonate (Calcite and aragonite) - Silica (crystalline quartz) - Calcium phosphate - cellulous,chitin,spongin

organisms with no hard parts rarely fossilized under special conditions

Modes of preservation

  • Unaltered: simple burial, some weathering
    • Becomes rare further back in fossil record
  • Permineralization - pour space is filled with a mineral from groundwater (Commonly silica or calcite)
  • Recrystalization - after burial, calcite crystals reorder and gtrow into one another (original mineralogy remains structure lost
  • Carbonization - Organics are compressed and distilled, leaving behind a carbon film.
  • Trace fossils - The record of an organisms behavior in the fossil record:
    • Trace fossils are essentially biogenerated sedimentary structures
    • Preserve like body fossils, in that they need to be rapidly buried
    • often found in env where body fossils are rare (due to baises in dep env.
