    Permian

    298Ma - 251 Ma (Triassic) After collapse of carboniferous rainforest ecosystem, vast deserts occupied much of the continental interior

    • World was dominated by supercontinent Pangea
  • Amniotes diversified during this time, leading to the ancestral groups of mammals, turtles, lepidosaurs and archosaurs
    • During this time, all the Earths major landmasses were in Pangaea
    • Deserts were widespread, with the interior of the continent experiencing extreme climate variation:
      • highly seasonal, with monsoonal conditions dominating the coastlines
    • Arid conditions favored gymnosperms over ferns and lycopods
      • Plants with seeds enclosed in a protective cover over those that need water to complete their lifecycle
  • Coal swamps decline throughout much of the world:
    • China and high latitude regions are exceptions
  • Glossopteris (of continental drift fame) was a seed fern that adapted to cold, dry environments
  • Conifers diversity
  • No New groups of arthropods appear during the permian
    • Giant forms disappear, likely due to a decrease in oxygen
  • Tetrapods four footed nibbas
    • Name is restricted to amphibians and amniota and all descendants of their most recent common ancestor
  • major ecological breakthrough breakthrough in the late carbo.:
    • amniotic egg:
      • Self continued egg with leathery shell, and tissues keep embryo moist, oxygenated and fed
    • Led to massive adaptive radiations
    • Amniotes become dominant group of large bodied terrestrial animals
  • Amniotes 2 groups:
    • Synapsids - enlarged jaw muscle opening for increased chewing power
    • Sauropsids - better color vision and water retention
  • Synapsids were first group to radiate during the early permian:
    • Small headed, heavily built herbivores
    • Semi aquatic fish eaters
    • Sail-backed plant and/or mollusk eaters
    • Sail-backed carnivores
  • early synapsids have sprawling stance found in more primitive tetrapods
  • Probably cold-blooded:
    • Cold-bloocdedness is the ancestral state of vertebrates
    • Some evidence they had parental care
    • Traditionally, these animals were considered reptiles:
      • They lack the shared derived characters of reptiles
      • Places in a separate group called the pelycosaurs, literally primitive synapsids
    • The early synapsids evolved themselves into extinction:
      • were replaced in the middle-late permian by the therapsids
    • Therapsida - advanced synapsids known informally as the 'mammal-like reptiles':
      • Greatly expanded infratemporal fenestra
      • Teeth divided into incisors, canines, and grinding back teeth
      • Forelimbs more powerfully developed than hind limbs
    • Predators, herbivores and omnivores appeared

    Therapsids

    • Ancestrally all vertebrates are cold-blooded, however, some evidence suggests the therapsids may have had elevated metabolisms:
      • This suggests they were at least partially warm-blooded
    • Some of the evidence includes:
      • A more upright stance than typical sprawling tetrapods
      • Some had a diaphragm, allowing for more effective breathing
      • Some had a secondary palate, allowing them to breath while feeding
      • Complex teeth for processing food more effectively
    • Ectotherms - an animal that is dependent on external source for body heat:
      • this is much less metabolically expensive, but it means that you are less active overall
      • Limits the kinds of environments or times of day and year when they can be active
    • Endotherms - an animal that is dependent on, or capable of the internal generation of heat:
      • This is due to mitochondria, and results in greater activity
      • Grow faster, quick recovery and live in colder climates
      • Tradeoff is, you need to eat a lot more
    • Relatively rare in the carb and permian
    • Characterized by a number features:
      • aglandular skin - few sweat glands
      • Waterproof skin - keratin makes it resistant
      • Water conserving kidneys - waste released as urea
      • Excellent color vision - 4-5 types of color recptors
    • These features would serve sauropsids well when the permo-triassic extinction hit

    Permo triassic mass extinction

    • Largest mass extinction of all time, with 80-96 species surviving(4-20%)
    • Victims:
      • Trilobites
      • Rugosa,tabulata
      • Graptolites
      • eurypterids
      • many brachiopods
      • blastoids
      • many primitive therapsids
    • This extinction reorganized the world
    • After this, the paleozoic fauna becomes subordinate to the modern marine fauna
    • Size and diversity of animals present is greatly reduced
    • There are some observable patterns to the organisms that do survive:

    Marine Realm

    • light calcifiers or non-calcifiers survive better
    • motile survive over sessile
    • organisms with protected respiration organs survive
    • Shifts the world to one dominated more by swimming and predation more than sessile
  • Continental
    • Survivors are adapted to low oxygen (burrowers, mountain dwellers, semi aquatic forms)
    • Favored those with sophisticated respiration or lower metabolic rates and small size
