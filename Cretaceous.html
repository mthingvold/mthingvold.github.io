Cretaceous Besides dinosaurs

  • lots of other stuff that happened besides radiation of birds/dinosuars
  • Pterosaurs, crocodilians, turtles, amphibians, and freshwater fish continue to diversify
  • The cretaceous Terrestrial revolution - diversification of herbaceous flowering plants, their pollinators, and small predators (lizards, snakes, mammals)

Evolution of Angiosperms

  • Before the cretaceous, the types of plants dominating the landscape were lycopods, cycads, different pine/spruce trees (gymnosperms), which rely on water or wind to disperse pollen/seeds
  • The flowering plants which are common today, did not show up until mid-late cretaceous
  • Angiosperm - A plant that has flowers and produces seeds encased within a fruit, produced from the flower.
    • All plants that produce a flower are an angiosperm
  • Flowering plants (angiosperms) May have appeared in jurassic, more important in cretaceous
  • basic angiosperm life cycle hinges on co-evolution with animals
  • Angiosperms developed an extraordinary mechanism for dispersing their seeds:
    • believed angiosperms able to evolve so rapidly due to co-evolution with insect Pollinators
    • enables spread of genetic material over much larger area than other plants.
  • Cretaceous angiosperms have fossil flowers that have features that are associated with insect pollination
  • basic angiosperm life cycle:
    • Bright colors, attractive smells, interesting patterns on the flower attract pollinators (typically flying insects), move pollen to flower of other plants.
    • Fruit remains bitter, hard, dull colored until the seeds are mature enough to grow.
      • At that point, fruit becomes brightly colored, fleshy, and sweet. Fruit eaten -> deposited as dung

Insect Radiation

  • Corresponding with diversification of angiosperms, radiation of insects:
    • for many groups, such as beetles (Coleoptera) and butterflies/moths (Lepidoptera) no coincidence
  • First flies Dipteta appear in triassic
  • First wasps hymenoptera) appear in the jurassic and bees and ants in the cretaceous
  • Beetles (Coleoptera) roaches, mantids and termites (Blattaria) and most other appear in the cretaceous

Sea info

  • Radiation of microscopic marine life in cretaceous:
    • Diatoms
    • Plankton
  • Reef building bivalves (Rudists) appear and become dominant reef builders
  • Ichthyosaurs become rare, die out before end of Cret.
  • Plesiosaurs arise (both long and short-necked forms
  • Sea turtles and Mosasuars appear

Chicxulub impact

  • Caculated probable size:
    • 10-15Km in diameter
    • Short term impact:
      • 1.8e8 megatons of energy released
      • vaporized anything within many km around
      • Shock wave generated huge tsunamis
    • Long term effect:
      • material vaporized by impact is kicked up into the atmosphere reducing sunlight
      • Dust and ash would block out the sun, killing off photosynthesizing organisms
      • This would have then killed off herbivores and finally carnivores
