Everything learned has been review calculus starts here at the heart of calculus is a new concept the limit of a function

lim f(x) x->a

asks the question: "What is the limit of f(x) as x approaches a?

"x approaches a" means that we must consider a progression of numbers getting closer and closer to the number a e.g If a= 5 then consider x=4.5, x=4.9 x=4.9999 a series of numbers that get closer without actually getting there.

x-> a^- means x approaches a from the left x->a^+ means x approaches a from the right x never touches a

x->a means x is approaching a but from both sides

lim f(x) asks "as x takes values x->a closer and closer to the number a, does f(x) also get closer to the number L if yes then we say that the limit exists and limx->a f(x) = L if no then we say that the limit does not exist
