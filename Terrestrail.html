    Desert - Landscape or region of land that is very dry due to low rainfall.

    • Often has little coverage by plants.
    • Steams dry up seasonally unless supplied by an outside area
    • More water lost due to evaporation
    • Soil has little organic matter
    • Soil is made up of evaporite minerals and eolian sand dunes
    • Precipitation does not leave the basin
    • Playa lakes are temporary lakes that are associated with evaporates
    • Steambeds are ephemeral and usually only run during heavy rainfall
    • normally >4 inches of rain/year
    • Practically all steambeds are dry most of the time and are ephemeral:
      • Carry water only during rainfall
      • Rain can be heavy and not all of it may soak into the ground
    • Desert vegatitive cover is sparse, runoff is largely unhindered and flash floods are common:
      • Streams lack extensive system of tributaries
      • Streams are small; die before reaching the sea
    • Dunes
      • Piles of sand moved downwind by wind direction
      • Forms through cross-bedding
    • Low-latitiue deserts:
      • Subtropics - dry climate

    • Mid-Latitude deserts
      • Located in the deep interiors of continents
      • High mountains in the path of the prevailing winds produce a rainshadow desert
      • precipitation only 10-20 cm/yr
    • Polar deserts
      • Many Polar regions are technically deserts because they receive little precipitation
      • precipitation only 10-20 cm

    Glaciers

    Glaciers: Rock cycle and hydrology cycle

    • Form where precipitation falls at high elevations or high latitudes

    Glacier: A thick mass of ice that originates on land from the accumulation, compaction, and recrystallization of snow

    Slightly more than 2 percent of the world's water in glaciers

    Antarctic ice sheet

    • 80 percent of the world's ice
    • Nearly two-thirds of earth's fresh water
    • Covers an area about the size of Canada
    • If melted, sea level would rise 60 to 70 metres

    Landforms Created by Glacial Erosion

    • Glacial movement is referred to as flow, and are capable of transporting large amounts of sediment.
      • Cirque - rock bowl at the head o a glaciated valley
      • Tarn - small lake in a cirque
      • Col - mountain pass between two cirques
      • Fjord - water-filled glacial trough along a coast
      • Arêtes - sharp edged ridges
      • Horns - pyramid-shaped mountain peaks

    *Deltas*

    • A prism of sediment that forms where a river enters a marine environments:
      • Mixture of marine and fluvial influences and are constructive
    • Abrupt decrease in energy causes river to quickly drop its sediment load
      • Sediments become finer as you farther into the lake or ocean
    • Consist of three subenvironments:
      • Delta Plain
      • Delta Front
      • Prodelta - seaward extent of delta influence
  • Mississipi river delta
    • Active lobe
      • Growing portion of delta
      • Switched lobes in the past
    • Abandoned lobe
      • Sediments compact
      • Lobe sinks
      • new lobe forms on top

    Estuaries Seaward portion of a drown valley system which is influenced by both marine and fluvial processes.

    • Brackish water environment
    • Tripartite Division Bayhead delta - Sande Central Lagoon/Basin - Mud Barrier Island - Sand
