Precambrian

Composed of several time periods and two eons:

  • Hadean
  • Archean (4-2.5Ga)
  • Proterozoic (2.5Ga-542Ma)
    • Ediacaran period spans last of proterozoic

Other time periods are occupied by a vast expanse of near nothingness It has been suggested that the precambrian be divided according to planetary events – Accretion and differentiation – moon impact – Hadean – dominated by heavy bombardment from extraterrestrial impacts – Archean – First crustal formations and increased atmospheric oxygen (BIFs) – Transition – first redbeds, and the end of BIFs – Proterozoic – Modern plate tectonics and thefirst animals

Relatively little is known about life in the Precambrian, despite the fact that it makes up 7/8 earth history

  • fossils rare
  • partially due to metamorphosis
  • others have been destroyed by erosion
  • Tracing the origins of life is therefore very difficult

Evidence of plate tectonics is largely unknown from the Precambrian, but it's assumed that plates did move, at least rudimentarily

  • Most landmass existed as a supercontinent known as Rodinia 1.1Ga to 750 Ma

First evidence of glaciers 2.2 Ga

Snowball Earth

Break-up of Rodina leaves many smaller continents near the equator Climate changed weather patterns, carbon dioxide sucked out of atmosphere Temperature drops to reduced greenhouse, which causes pack ice to build on the poles

  • Positive feedback loops causes ice to spread

Hydrologic cycle stops, ice thickens to as much as 1km, oceans become anoxic

  • Life clings to refuges in ice cracks and along mid ocean ridges
  • Glaciers stop building

Cambrian Period

Disappearance of ediacaran biota and end of ediacaran period marks the start of the cambrian

The cambrian is the first geo period of the paleozoic era starting 541 Ma and ending 485 Ma

Cambrian marked by profound change in fauna = Cambrian explosion Subsequent end of the cambrian marked by mass extinction which began around 490 Ma

Most continental land was clustered in the southern hemisphere over the south pole formed supercontinent cut off ocean circulation, made colder time period avg 7 degrees warmer than today Marked by dramatic increase in biodiversity

  • Coincided with an increase in burrowing complexity and behavior which transformed seabed ecosystems dramatically

Almost all modern animal phyla appeared during this time

  • many ogranisms don't belong to any modern group

Many organisms soft-bodied, some shelly today more than 2/3 of soft-bodied groups never found in fossils Few cambrian fossil beds that preserver soft-bodied organisms

Cambrian explosion Event lasted 20-25Ma several stages

  1. Diversification of biomineralizing animals and deep, complex burrows
  2. Radiation of molluscs and brachiopods
  3. Diversification of trilobites

Contention as to whether cambrian explosion was as dramatic as originally conceived

  • does seem innovations which appeared only evolved once
  • Statistical evidence suggest the radiation was no faster, but appears that way due to thie appearance of mineralized body parts

Regardless of how explosive this event was the pace of evolution was fast, and several theories for this have been suggested:

  • Further increase in oxygen
  • Increase CaCO₂ in water
  • Ozone formation
  • Emergence of predator-prey dynamic
  • Evolution of eyes

Regardless this all came to a rather crashing end with the cambrian-ordovician mass extinction

  • these organisms that survived further radiated in the ordovician, leading to the modern groups we see today

During the cambrian explosion, we have the first record of trilobites, stem mollusks, gastropods, ibvalves, echinoderms, vertebrates and other oddities Critical time for diversification of life with the appearance of all modern animal phyla critical significance: biomineralization

What was the cambrian explosion?

  • A phylogenetic event?
    • Based on a literal reading of the fossils as an actual sudden origin of these lineages
  • developmental event?
    • change in genetic architecture allowing for biomineralization, either by new genes or new interaction of genes.
  • ecological event?
    • Interaction between organisms promoted mineralized skeletons
  • Biogeochem event
    • change in external conditions could've allowed biomineralization

Why Biomineralization?

We can reject a literal phylogenetic model (and therefore a molecular model), as it is known that groups such as Cnidarians and Bilaterians (Kimberella) had appeared in the Ediacaran It's known that the development and scale of biomineralization increased dramatically from ediacaran to the cambrian: Increased oxygen levels allowing for higher metabolic rates and larger body size Changing water chemistry allowing for easier production of calcium carbonate Further supported by fossil evidence

Kimberella is completely unmineralized Cambrian Odontogriphus has mineralized radula Cambrian Wiwaxia has a mineralized radula and kertinized sclerites Halkeriids have a mineralized radula & calcified sclerites Early mollusks had shells
