Abstraction

  • Eliminate irrelevant details
  • ignore relevant details that aren't needed for the current task
  • generalize the problem to handle more situations and allow its reuse

Decomposition

  • Most software systems are very complex
  • Often the key to solving a complex problem is decomposition
    • Divid complex problems into smaller problems
    • solve small problems
    • Combine the results of sub to solve orginal
  • Subproblems are solved by recursively applying the approach, until problems are obtained that are simle enough to directly solve.
  • Use abstraction to ignore details of how subproblem is solved
  • Need spec of what the subproblem does
  • For a method, the spec consists of
    • signature
  • precondition
  • postcondition (including return value)
