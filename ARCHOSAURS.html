Archosaurs

  • Archosaurs – a group of diapsid amniotes whose living representatives consist of non – avian dinosaurs, crocodiles, and birds
  • Are defined by having an extra opening between the eye socket and the nostril, possibly holding soft tissue associated with cooling the face and breathing
  • Can be divided into two main groups;
    • Pseudosuchia – crocodilians and everything more closely related to crocodilians than to birds
    • Ornithodira – birds and non-avian dinosaurs
    • Sister group is the Phytosaurs, a group of crocodile-like reptiles
  • Archosaurs are classified into the two main groups of Pseudosuchia and Ornithodira based on their ankle joints
  • The Pseudosuchia retained an ankle structure found in earlier reptiles
  • The Ornithodira have ankles like birds, allowing them to move their ankle in a single plane, like a hinge
  • This was more suitable for running
  • Early Archosaurs had a sprawling gait, due to the placement of their ankles and hips
  • As time went on, Archosaurs began to evolve past this
  • The pseudosuchia dominated mid-late triassic
  • Radiated into a number of forms:
    • armored spiky herbivores
    • Herbivorous quadrupeds
    • toothless bipeds
    • Terrestrial carnivours
    • Fast-running quadrupeds:
      • oddly enough, this line produces the crocodilian lineage
  • The Pseudosuchians include some of the first terrestrial animals to exceed the size of hippos

Ornithodira

  • The Ornithodira appear in the fossil record in the mid Tri but there are footprints that suggest they evolved in the early triassic
  • More lightly built than pseudosuchians, and most were originally bipedal
  • This group is made up of the first proto - dinosaurs, true dinosaurs, and their closest relatives:
    • together, they are known as the dinosauromorphs
    • Defined on the basis of their hindlimbs:
      • erect posture
      • hinge like ankles and digitigrade posture

Locomotion

  • in the mid Triassic, some archosaurs developed hip joints that required them to have a more erect gait:
    • this allowed them to have greater stamina due to evolving beyond Carrier's constraint
    • Carrier's constraint - air breathing vertebrates which have two lungs and flex their bodies sideways during locomotion find it difficult to breath at the same time:
      • This means archosaurs could runk and breath at the same time
  • An upright stance requires more energy and is therefore metabolically expensive

Metabolism

  • Archosaurs metabolism is a topic of intense interest:
    • how can archosaurs maintain a posture and lifestyle that is metabolically expensive?
    • We can look to Crocodiles to provide some of these answers
  • 4 - Chambered Heart:
    • Both mammals and birds have a 4-chambered heart, which completely separates the flow of oxygenated and deoxygenated blood:
      • reptiles have a 3 chambered heart
    • Modern crocodiles have a 4 chambered heart, and a pulmonary bypass
  • Secondary palate:
    • An anatomical structure that divides the nasal cavity from the oral cavity
    • This allows an animal to eat and breath at the same time
    • Also protects their brain from struggling prey
  • Hepatic Piston
    • Archosaurs had a muscular diaphragm for breathing, much like mammals have
    • Different in that it connects to the front of the pelvis, which pulls the liver down so the lungs can expand
    • Air flows unidirectional through the lungs
  • All of these features combined suggest that Archosaurs were actually warm-blooded
  • Then why are crocodiles ectothermic?
    • The anatomical evidence shows that crocodiles were originally warm blooded, but reverted back to a cold-blooded lifestyle when it became an aquatic ambush predator
    • The earliest crocodilians were slim, leggy terrestrial predators. Some were even bipedal.
    • Erect limbs are beneficial for active predators, and crocodiles do retain this to a degree

Waste Removal

  • Therapsids were still around during the Triassic, were well established and were the dominant terrestrial organisms, so why did they disappear?
  • Partly, this is due to waste removal, and how these groups excrete ammonia:
    • Ammonia is a byproduct of metabolism, and is created through amino acid breakdown and is toxic in high amounts
    • In reptiles and birds, uric acid is excreted in the feces as a dry waste
    • In Synapdis, therapsids and mammals, it is excreted as urea as a wet waste
  • Due to paleoclimate and geography the fact the archosaurs don't pee and have aglandulous favoured their success over early mammals
