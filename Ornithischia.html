Ornithischia

Thyreophora

  • Name means 'shield bearers' known as armored dinos
  • covered in armor grown from skin
  • hindlimbs longer than forelimbs
  • broad subgroups
  • Ankylosaurs - bulky quadrupeds with armor covering their bodies and large spines and clubs:
    • more common in cretaceous
  • Stegosaurs - identified by plates and spikes along their back and shoulders:
    • more common in the jurassic

Ornithopoda

Name means 'bird foot', small facultative bipedal running grazers:

  • most successful group of herbivores in cretaceous
  • Includes hadrosaurs, which are more commonly known as the duck-billed dinosaurs:
    • Jaws were designed for grinding plants, multiple rows of teeth being replaced continuously throughout their lifetime
    • Elaborate head ornamentation in the form of crests

    Marginocephalia

    • Name means 'margin head' and are a group thatis characterized by a bony shelf or margin at the back of the skull
      • Likely used as display/combat
    • Two main subgroups:
      • Pachycephalosaurs - thick headed lizards, bipedal thought to be combative
      • Ceratopsians - "horned faces" were a group of quadrupedal beaked herbivores with elaborate skull ornamentation.
