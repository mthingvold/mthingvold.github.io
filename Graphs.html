A graph G consists of

  • a set of vertices, V(G)
  • a set of edges E(G) Each edge is associated with either one or two vertices called its endpoints. An edge connects its endpoints. A vertex is incident to an edge if the vertex is an endpoint of the edge. two vertices connected by an edge are adjacent

An edge with only one endpoint is a loop, and two or more edges with the same endpoints are said to be parallel. Simple graph won't contain these

Handshake theorem: The sum of the degrees of all the vertices of a graph G is equal to twice the number of edges in G. Proof: By induction on the number of edges in the graph = m Basis case: m=0 In a graph with no edges, the sum of the degrees of all the vertices in the graph is zero, which is also twice the number

Cycle A cycle in a graph is a simple circuit that contains at least one edge.

Trees A tree is a graph which is connected, but contains no cycle. A forest is a graph which contains no cycle.

Theorem: If a tree T = (V,E) has n>1 vertices, then T has at least 2 vertices of degree 1.

Proof: By induction on n. Basis Case: n=2 The only tree with two vertices consists of the two vertices connected by an edge.

Inductive Hypothesis: Assume that all trees with at least 2, but at most k verses have at least 2 vertices of degree 1.

Inductive step: consider a tree T with n=k+1 vertices. Let e = (u,v) be an edge in T.

Proceed by cases: Case 1: Both u and v are of degree 1. This case cannot occur as T has at least three vertices, and it could not be connected if two adjacent vertices were of degree one. Case 2: Exactly one of u or v are of degree 1. Assume u is of degree 1. Let Tv be the subtree of T

Rooted trees A rooted tree is a tree with one vertex designated as the root of the tree The level of avertex u in a rooted tree is the number of edges along the unique path between u and the root. The height of a rooted tree is the maximum level of a vertex in the tree parent: for each vertex u, except the root, of a rooted tree the vertex adjacent to u, but at level one less than that of u is the parent of u. Child: for a vertex u in a rooted tree, the vertices adjacent to u but at level one greater than u are the children of u leaf: a vertex in a rooted tree with no children is called a leaf.

Binary Trees: A binary tree is a rooted tree in which every parent has at most 2 children.
