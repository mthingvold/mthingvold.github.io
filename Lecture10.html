Shortcut to find the horizontal asymptotes of a rational function f(x) = p(x)/q(x)

  1. if the degree of q(x)>degree of p(x) then there is a horizontal asymptote at y=0
  2. if the degree of q(x) = degree of p(x) then there is a horizontal asymptote at y=a/b (where a is the coefficient of the highest powered term of p and b is the coefficient of the highest powered term of q)
  3. if the degree of q(x) < degree of p(x) you don't have a horizontal asymptote. (if you need to find the infinite limits, make a small table of values)
