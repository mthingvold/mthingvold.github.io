The Architecture of Knowledge

Foundationalism: A body of knowledge is held together by its foundations(ie a set of self-justificatory beliefs)

Coherentism: what holds a body of knowledge together is the coherence of its element (the fit between beliefs.)

descartes strategy: first med: demolition work through doubt 2 med: laying the first building block in the cogito argument 3 med: building foundation with the criterion for certainty

finding criterion for certainty: p.24 descartes meditations

descarte's argument

  1. main preimse: i have a clear perception of what i'm asserting
  2. intermediary premise: If what i perceive clearly and distinctly can eb falsae then clarity and distinctnes cannot be the criterion for certainty
  3. Conclusion: i can now determine my criterion for certainty: whatever i perceive very clearly and very distinctly is true.
